About Bacchanalia

Where Passion Meets Tradition

Clement Filewod trained at the Stratford Culinary School in Ontario and went on to work in professional kitchens where he developed a straightforward approach to cooking: good ingredients, honest technique, and dishes that make sense on the plate. Bacchanalia is his first restaurant and the place where that vision finally has a home.

The name comes from the ancient Roman festival dedicated to Bacchus, god of wine and celebration. It captures the kind of experience Clement wanted to create: somewhere that feels festive without being stiff, where the food is the focus and guests feel welcome to stay.

The menu draws from classical technique and seasonal ingredients, with dishes that are thoughtful without being overcomplicated. Every item on the plate has a purpose.

Bacchanalia is rooted in the idea that a great meal is more than the food itself. It is the setting, the people around the table, and the feeling that something memorable happened that night.
