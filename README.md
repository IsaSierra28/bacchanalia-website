# bacchanalia-website
Frontend Development 

What the Website Is About 
Bacchanalia is the website for a future restaurant and pub that takes its name from the 
ancient Roman festival, a celebration of wine, theater and joy in honor of Bacchus, the god 
of wine. The site will work as the main online presence of this place, showing visitors what 
the restaurant is about and what kind of experience they can expect. The purpose is to inform 
people about the concept, build excitement before the opening and establish a strong identity 
that feels both elegant and welcoming at the same time. 

Motivation and Goals 
This website is personal to me because its based on the dream of someone very close to 
me. He studied at the Stratford Culinary School in Ontario and after working in professional 
kitchens for a while he now wants to open his own restaurant. He wants to have his own 
business and create a space that truly reflects his passion for food and for culture, specially 
the Roman world. He picked the name Bacchanalia because it represents exactly the kind of 
vibe he wants for the place, festive and a little theatrical. My goal with this website is to help 
him promote his vision and also to put in practice everything I have been learning in this 
course. I want the site to tell his story in a way that feels genuine and also looks really 
professional. 
Sections of the Website 
The website will be organized in the following sections: 
• Home, with a hero section that shows the name of the restaurant, a short tagline and 
a button to explore more. 
• About, where we tell the story of the chef, his training at Stratford Culinary School and 
the inspiration behind the name. 
• Menu, showing a preview of the food and drinks that the restaurant will offer. 
• Gallery, with photos of the food, the space and the overall atmosphere. 
• Team, with short profiles of the people behind the restaurant. 
• Contact, with the address, hours, a way to make reservations and how to reach us. 
Design Ideas 
For the design he wants something that feels neutral and elegant but not too cold or stiff. The 
colors will be mostly dark tones like charcoal and off white with some gold details that remind 
you of something classy and a bit ancient. I think that combination that works for a restaurant 
that wants to feel special without being intimidating. For the fonts I want to mix a serif font for 
the titles and something cleaner for the regular text. The images should be moody and well 
lit, the kind of photos that make food looks good. The layout will be simple and organized.

with a lot of white space nothing feels too crowded. A website I made before for this course 
inspired me a lot for this project because I liked how it used card grids and dark sections and 
I showed that to him and he liked it. I decided to use that as a starting point. 
Possible Domain Names 
• www.bacchanalia.ca because it is short, easy to remember and connects directly to 
the name of the restaurant. 
• www.bacchanaliarestaurant.com as a more complete option that also makes clear 
what kind of place it is. 
• www.dine-bacchanalia.com as a friendlier version that feels more like an invitation to 
come and visit.
